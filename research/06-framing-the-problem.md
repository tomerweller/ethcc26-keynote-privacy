# Privacy on Open Blockchains: Framing the Problem
**Author:** Marcelo Prates | **Source:** stellar.org

## The Core Challenge

Consumers risk exposing transaction history and balances. Institutions risk disclosing competitive business intelligence.

## Pseudonymity vs Privacy

Blockchain addresses are alphanumeric strings with no inherent identity. The privacy problem emerges when blockchain data combines with external information sources. Regulated entities must link onchain addresses to real-world identities (AML/CTF), making this worse.

## The "Self-Doxxing" Problem

During transactions, parties involuntarily reveal addresses to each other. Either party can then examine the address's complete history and balances -- "breaking confidentiality by self-doxxing."

## Two Critical Questions for Solutions

1. **Who provides the privacy mechanism?** For regulated assets like stablecoins, issuers are natural candidates. For non-regulated tokens, external solutions like privacy pools.
2. **Who discloses confidential information for compliance?** Regulatory disclosure responsibilities remain key.
