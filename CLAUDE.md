# Privacy That Doesn't Break the Chain

## Project
EthCC talk (max 20 min) on blockchain privacy for institutions. The core thesis: we've been approaching privacy wrong for institutional users. Privacy is not all-in or all-out -- it's a spectrum. Solutions must preserve the transparency and openness that make blockchains valuable in the first place.

## Audience
Mid-technical developers and institutions. They understand blockchain basics and can follow technical concepts but don't need deep ZK math.

## Key Framing
- "Doesn't break the chain" = doesn't break transparency/openness of blockchains
- Institutions have unique privacy needs distinct from retail/consumer privacy
- Privacy is a spectrum, not a binary toggle
- The crypto ecosystem has been building privacy with cypherpunk values, not institutional/practical requirements
- It's not institutions getting it wrong -- it's crypto builders ignoring what institutions actually need
