# Your Paycheck Is Private. So, Why Isn't Your Blockchain Payment?
**Author:** James Bachini | **Source:** stellar.org

## The Problem

In traditional finance, privacy is the default. On blockchain, anyone with a block explorer can view everything.

History: first peer-to-peer electronic cash focused on transparency above all. Pseudonymity became the default -- "a temporary mask that slips the moment someone looks closely enough."

"Without privacy, it is impossible to meet compliance requirements, protect competitive advantage, or maintain basic end-user expectations."

## The Solution (2026+)

Protocol X-Ray upgrade introduces BN254 curves and Poseidon hashing to Soroban, enabling ZK proof verification in Stellar smart contracts.

Key capability: prove data attributes without revealing underlying information. Example: confirm an address has adequate funds without disclosing the balance.

## Vision

"Privacy and transparency aren't going to be opposites; we will use these tools to create new systems that provide personal financial privacy while retaining the advantages and transparency of a public blockchain."
